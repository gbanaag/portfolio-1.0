BRIEF

Trill is a peer support network.

Trill Project is a social networking platform that creates a virtual support community with a focus on inclusivity, privacy, and anonyminity. Users can anonymously express themselves and connect with other users on the application.

Trill was acquired by Blue Fever in late 2021. The app is no longer accessible to the public.

THE PROBLEM

Users wanted us to expand how to connect on Trill.

Trill's users had expressed that they wanted to find more ways to connect with users. While the app supported direct messaging other users, finding users through posts, and joining communities, through monthly surveys Trill's beta testers expressed wanting a group chat feature to be able to connect with more people at once through mutual friends and interests.

DELIVERABLES AND APPROACH

I was tasked with designing a new feature for the app - the ability to create group chats with other users. This was my first time working on prototyping a new feature so I was unsure to go about doing so, but here's what I ended up doing.

Inspiration has always guided me as a designer.

My bigger strengths as a designer reside in visual and creative design, and the start of my creative process goes to looking at inspiration and taking examples to make them my own. While I didn't have a lot of creative leeway on this project, I took a similar approach on this task.

I went through a couple of mobile apps that I used that had group chat features, and took note of features, interactions, and functions that they had in common. From there, I worked on a couple of both low-fidelity and high-fidelity wireframes to arrange where to put the app's components.


Common group chat features

  • Group chat names
  • Buttons for group chat info
  • Buttons to add group members
  • Area to type and send messages
